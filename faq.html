How does it work, and what is identity verification?

First, you need to know that identity verification guarantees the existence of a real, natural person behind any process.

It proves through different identity verification methods that the user is who they claim to be. Moreover, it is very important to underline that it is required by law and prevents a person from carrying out a process in the name of another without authorization and from using false identities to commit fraud (among these, for example, impersonation, money laundering, etc.).

What is the difference between KYC and KYB?

They are two similar but different concepts: KYC verifies the identity of the end customer (or user), while KYB verifies the company's identity. KYB is a process that can be used at the corporate level, between company and company. On the other hand, KYC is responsible for verifying the identities of a company's customers.

It is important to mention that the objective of these processes is the same: to fight fraud, ensure cybersecurity and thus prevent cyber attacks or, also, money laundering.

At Silt, we offer both solutions: KYC to verify the identity of your customers and KYB to verify companies and freelancers. Contact us: hello@getsilt.com.

Is biometric facial recognition secure?

Biometric facial recognition systems use unique mathematical patterns to store user data.

Facial data can be anonymized and kept private to reduce the risk of unauthorized access. In addition, life activity detection technology distinguishes between real users and their facial images.

It should be added that according to Incode, facial recognition is the most recommended biometric system: it is 25 times more secure than fingerprinting.

Silt is a cloud-based tool, and we work with unstructured biometric data related to videos, photos and audio to create reusable digital identities of users.
